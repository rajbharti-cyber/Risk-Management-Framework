# 📈 Risk Scoring Methodology

## 📌 Overview

This document outlines a sample methodology for evaluating and prioritizing cyber and technology risks based on likelihood and business impact.

The purpose of this methodology is to support:
- Consistent risk evaluation
- Governance reporting
- Risk-informed decision-making
- Remediation prioritization
- Audit and compliance activities

---

# 🎯 Risk Assessment Approach

Risks are evaluated using two primary dimensions:

| Factor | Description |
|---|---|
| Likelihood | Probability of the risk occurring |
| Impact | Business effect if the risk materializes |

The combination of these factors determines overall risk severity.

---

# 📊 Likelihood Ratings

| Rating | Description |
|---|---|
| Low | Unlikely to occur |
| Medium | Possible occurrence |
| High | Likely or frequent occurrence |

---

# 📊 Impact Ratings

| Rating | Description |
|---|---|
| Low | Minimal operational impact |
| Medium | Noticeable operational or compliance impact |
| High | Significant business, financial, or regulatory impact |

---

# 🚨 Risk Severity Matrix

| Likelihood | Impact | Overall Risk |
|---|---|---|
| Low | Low | Low |
| Low | Medium | Low |
| Medium | Medium | Medium |
| High | Medium | High |
| High | High | Critical |

---

# 🛡️ Residual Risk Consideration

Residual risk represents the remaining exposure after existing controls are applied.

Examples of controls include:
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA)
- Access reviews
- SIEM monitoring
- Vulnerability management
- Logging and alerting
- Security awareness training

---

# 📋 Governance Considerations

Risk scoring should support:
- Business-aligned prioritization
- Regulatory expectations
- Audit defensibility
- Executive reporting
- Remediation tracking

---

# 🧭 Example Risk Prioritization

| Risk Example | Severity |
|---|---|
| Excessive privileged access | High |
| Missing audit logs | Medium |
| Unsupported legacy systems | Critical |
| Delayed security patching | High |

# 🔍 Control Gap Assessment

## 📌 Overview

This document demonstrates a sample control gap assessment performed from a Cyber Risk and Technology Risk perspective.

The purpose of this assessment is to identify weaknesses in existing security and operational controls, evaluate associated business risks, and recommend remediation actions.

---

# 🎯 Assessment Objectives

- Evaluate effectiveness of existing controls
- Identify governance and security gaps
- Assess operational and regulatory impact
- Support remediation planning
- Improve audit readiness

---

# 📊 Sample Control Gap Assessment

| Control Area | Observed Gap | Risk Impact | Recommendation | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Access Management | Privileged access reviews not consistently performed | Excessive access risk | Implement quarterly access reviews | High |
| Logging & Monitoring | Incomplete audit log retention | Reduced incident visibility | Enhance centralized logging retention | Medium |
| Patch Management | Delayed deployment of critical patches | Vulnerability exposure | Reduce patch remediation timelines | High |
| Third-Party Governance | Vendor assessments inconsistently documented | Third-party security risk | Standardize vendor review process | Medium |
| Incident Governance | Incident escalation procedures unclear | Delayed response coordination | Formalize incident escalation workflow | Medium |

---

# 🛡️ Governance Perspective

Control gaps can lead to:
- Audit findings
- Regulatory exposure
- Operational disruption
- Financial impact
- Weak accountability

This assessment focuses on governance and risk implications rather than deep technical exploitation.
